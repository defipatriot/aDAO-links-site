The Basics: Start Here

What is TLA?

üéØ Simple Version:

TLA is a rewards program. You deposit tokens into trading pools, and you earn weekly LUNA rewards based on community votes.

The Terra Liquidity Alliance (TLA) is a community-driven system that distributes LUNA rewards to people who provide trading liquidity on Terra.

Here's how it works step by step:

  1. You deposit pairs of tokens (like LUNA + USDC) into trading pools on exchanges like Astroport
  2. Your deposits let other people trade those tokens smoothly
  3. The community votes each week on which pools deserve rewards
  4. Pools with more votes get a bigger share of the weekly LUNA distribution
  5. You earn your portion based on how much you deposited

üè¨ Think of it like this: Imagine a shopping mall where store owners set up shops. The mall management distributes bonus money to stores based on customer votes. Popular, useful stores get more bonuses. That attracts more store owners. The mall gets better for everyone.

Why does this exist? Terra needs deep pools of tokens so traders can swap efficiently. Without liquidity, trades have bad prices (high "slippage"). TLA incentivizes people to provide this liquidity by sharing protocol rewards.

What is Liquidity?

üéØ Simple Version:

Liquidity is money sitting in a pool that lets other people trade. You deposit it, traders use it, and you earn fees.

When you want to swap Token A for Token B, someone needs to have Token B available to give you. Liquidity providers (LPs) deposit pairs of tokens into pools so traders can swap between them.

‚úàÔ∏è Think of it like this: A currency exchange booth at an airport needs to have dollars, euros, yen, etc. on hand to exchange for travelers. The money sitting in the booth is "liquidity". The booth earns a small fee on each exchange. When you provide liquidity, you're like that currency booth.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Risk:

Providing liquidity isn't risk-free. If token prices change significantly, you may experience "impermanent loss" - getting back a different ratio of tokens than you deposited. Research this before depositing significant amounts.

What is an Epoch?

üéØ Simple Version:

An epoch is a one-week cycle. Each week: people vote, votes are counted, and rewards are distributed. Then it starts over.

TLA operates in weekly cycles called epochs. Each epoch follows this pattern:

  1. Voting Period: Token holders allocate their voting power to pools they want to support
  2. Epoch End: Votes are tallied at the end of Monday (UTC)
  3. Reward Distribution: LUNA rewards flow to pools based on their vote share
  4. Repeat: The next epoch begins immediately

üí∞ Think of it like this: Like a weekly allowance that gets divided up. Every week, the family votes on how to split the allowance between different savings goals. At week's end, money goes into each goal based on the votes.

Epochs run Monday to Monday (UTC). The epoch number you see on this dashboard (like "Epoch 161") counts how many weeks have passed since TLA launched.

How Does Voting Work?

üéØ Simple Version:

You lock special LUNA tokens (ampLUNA) to get voting power. You point your votes at pools you like. Pools with more votes get more rewards.

To participate in voting, you need Voting Power (VP). Here's how to get it:

  1. Get ampLUNA or arbLUNA: Convert your LUNA through Eris Protocol or buy on a DEX
  2. Lock your tokens: Lock them on a platform like Votion. Longer locks = more VP per token
  3. Allocate votes: Use the Eris TLA interface to point your VP at pools
  4. Earn rewards: You get base TLA rewards, plus any bribes from projects seeking votes

üèõÔ∏è Think of it like this: Like shares in a company giving you votes at shareholder meetings. More shares = more say in decisions. In TLA, more locked tokens = more voting power.

Why lock tokens? Locking demonstrates long-term commitment. Someone who locks for 1 year gets more voting power than someone who can exit tomorrow. This encourages stable, thoughtful governance.

Glossary: Key Terms

VP Voting Power

How much voting weight someone has. More locked tokens = more VP. Determines your influence over which pools get rewards.

APR Annual Percentage Rate

Projected yearly return. A 50% APR on $1,000 = ~$500/year. But APR changes constantly based on deposits, votes, and prices.

Depth Pool Depth / Liquidity

Total money deposited in a pool. Deeper pools = better prices for traders. Like how much inventory a store has in stock.

Vol Trading Volume

Total value of trades through a pool (usually per day/week). High volume = busy pool. Volume generates trading fees for LPs.

Bribes Vote Incentives

Extra rewards offered to attract votes. Projects pay bribes to get their pools more votes. Vote for bribed pools = earn bribe share.

LSD Liquid Staking Derivative

Token representing staked LUNA (like ampLUNA). Earns staking rewards AND can be used in DeFi. Best of both worlds.

Slip Slippage

Getting worse price than expected. Big trades in small pools have high slippage. 1% slippage on $10K trade = $100 lost.

IL Impermanent Loss

Potential loss when providing liquidity if prices change. Called "impermanent" because it reverses if prices return. Real risk in volatile pools.

DEX Decentralized Exchange

Trading platform without middlemen. Astroport and Skeleton Swap are Terra's main DEXes. You trade directly with pools, not other users.

LP Liquidity Provider

Someone who deposits tokens into pools. That's you when you provide liquidity! LPs earn trading fees + TLA rewards.

Bucket Pool Category

TLA groups pools into buckets (STABLE, PROJECT, BLUECHIP, SINGLE) so similar pools compete fairly against each other.

Eff Efficiency

Volume √∑ Liquidity. Shows how "hard" the money in a pool is working. High efficiency = lots of trading relative to deposits.

KEY aDAO Score (Overall Score)

üéØ Simple Version:

The aDAO Score is our overall opportunity rating. Higher = pool is accessible, performing well, but maybe under-supported (meaning your votes would have more impact).

The aDAO Score combines three factors:

Access Score (15% weight)

How easy is it for a newcomer to participate? Can they find and acquire the tokens without getting confused?

Performance Score (55% weight)

Is the pool financially healthy? Good APR, decent volume, active trading?

Support Score (30% weight, inverted)

How much backing does it already have? Note: We invert this - well-supported pools need less help, so they score lower on opportunity.

aDAO Score = (Access √ó 0.15) + (Performance √ó 0.55) + ((100 - Support) √ó 0.30)

How to use it: Think of high aDAO Score as "worth investigating." The pool has good fundamentals but might be overlooked. Low scores might mean the pool is already well-covered or has access issues.

Performance Score

üéØ Simple Version:

How well is this pool doing financially? High APR, good volume, healthy depth = high performance score.

Performance Score evaluates the pool's financial health using:

Pools are compared within their bucket (STABLE pools vs other STABLE pools) so you're seeing performance relative to similar pools.

Support Score measures existing vote allocation. It's based on:

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm new to Terra. Where do I start?

Welcome! Here's a suggested path:

  1. Get a Cosmos wallet (Keplr or Leap are popular)
  2. Acquire some LUNA from an exchange
  3. Explore Astroport to see how swapping works
  4. Consider converting some LUNA to ampLUNA for staking rewards
  5. Once comfortable, explore providing liquidity

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning:

Never invest more than you can afford to lose. DeFi has real risks. Smart contracts can have bugs. Start small and learn before going big.

What's the "best" pool to deposit in?

There's no single "best" - it depends on your goals:

  • Want stability? LSD pools (LUNA/ampLUNA) have minimal impermanent loss
  • Want high yield? Check high APR pools, but understand the higher risk
  • Have stables? Stable pools (USDC/USDT) are low-risk but lower yield

Use our scores as a starting point, but always research each pool before depositing. Consider: your risk tolerance, assets you already hold, how long you'll stay deposited.

When do I receive rewards?

TLA rewards follow the epoch cycle:

  • Rewards are calculated at epoch end (Monday UTC)
  • Distribution happens shortly after (usually within hours)
  • LP rewards accrue but may take time to appear after transitions
  • Bribe rewards follow their own platform schedules

Important: Rewards don't auto-compound. You need to claim them periodically through the TLA interface.

Will I lose money to impermanent loss?

IL is a real risk, but it varies by pool type:

  • Minimal IL: Correlated pairs (LUNA/ampLUNA) - they move together
  • Low IL: Stable pairs (USDC/USDT) - both stay near $1
  • Higher IL: Uncorrelated pairs (LUNA/BTC) - prices can diverge significantly

High APR often compensates for IL, but not always. If worried, start with correlated or stable pairs.

How accurate are your scores?

Our scores are objective calculations based on public on-chain data. They're accurate for what they measure.

What scores CAN'T capture:

  • Smart contract risk (could the code have bugs?)
  • Governance risk (could the project rug?)
  • Team reliability (will they stick around?)
  • Future changes (will this APR last?)

Use scores as ONE tool among many. High score ‚â† "safe bet". Low score ‚â† "avoid".

How do I vote on TLA?

To participate in voting:

  1. Acquire ampLUNA or arbLUNA (swap on Astroport or mint through Eris)
  2. Lock them on Votion (or similar) - longer lock = more voting power
  3. Visit the Eris TLA interface
  4. Allocate your voting power to pools you want to support
  5. Optionally, vote for pools with bribes to earn extra rewards

Votes persist until you change them. You can reallocate anytime.
