Bounty Board

What pools are paying for votes right now. $ / 1M VP is the price of support — high means your vote is underpaid elsewhere. learn more

What this means: Projects put up rewards ("bribes") to attract votes to their pool this epoch. Every voter on that pool shares the pot in proportion to their votes. Dividing the pot by the pool's current votes gives the going rate — dollars paid per 1M VP of support. A high rate means few voters are competing for that pot: moving votes there pays more. A pool with a pot but almost no votes is the loudest signal on the board. Rates shift as votes move, right up to the epoch close.

Stale VP — power left on the table

VP decays until a lock is re-touched. This is the whole electorate's VP today vs what it would be if every lock were adjusted — and who's sitting on the biggest recoverable gap. learn more

What this means: vAMP voting power decays over time and snaps back to full strength when the lock is adjusted (extended or re-committed). A wallet that hasn't touched its locks in months is voting with less power than it owns — stale VP. The gap is free power: adjusting costs nothing but a transaction. The wallets below have the most to reclaim; if one is yours, go adjust. Group VP in the pie and boards uses displayed (current) VP, so reclaiming stale VP literally grows your slice.

Take-Rate Watch

Deposits sitting in INACTIVE pools keep paying the take rate while earning nothing — slowly eaten. learn more

Why this board exists: When a pool falls below the 1% vote threshold it stops earning rewards, but deposits left in it still pay the take rate — value quietly draining every epoch. The wallets below have money in that state. If a name is shown, that's a registered handle — anyone in the community can reach out and say "pull your deposit, it's being eaten." That's the point: this is a care board, not a shame board.

Is TLA Liquidity Growing?

Is the liquidity base expanding or shrinking — measured with price noise stripped out. learn more

What this means: Think of TLA as a big shared pot of crypto. This asks whether the pot is getting bigger or smaller. The catch: if LUNA's price drops, the pot looks smaller in dollars even if no one withdrew anything. So we also measure it a second way that ignores price changes — the "real" trend, the same reserves valued at today's prices. The chart shows both lines per epoch (indexed to 100 at the window start) so a one-week event reads as a step, not a permanent trend — and when one week explains most of the move, the verdict says exactly that. If real liquidity is flat or rising, the pot is healthy and money isn't fleeing; a falling dollar figure alongside a flat real line just means the market moved.

What a one-sided trade of this size costs in price impact, per pool, from live reserves. how it's estimated

The model: For standard (xyk) pools, impact = trade ÷ (input-side reserve + trade) — the exact constant-product execution-price formula, using the pool's real per-side balances, worst direction shown. Concentrated and stable pools bunch their liquidity near the current price, so their true impact is LOWER than this figure — those rows show "≤" and grade on the bound. This ignores fees, routing through other pools, and same-block competition; treat it as a planning number, not a quote. Grades: A under 0.5% · B under 1% · C under 3% · D under 8% · F above.

Threshold Watch

Pools need >1% of bucket votes to stay active and earn. Showing pools at risk and recently inactive. learn more

What this means: For a pool to earn rewards it needs at least 1% of the votes in its group (bucket). Below that line it goes "inactive" and stops earning — and a fee (take rate) slowly eats positions left sitting there. This panel flags pools close to the line (at risk of falling), pools already below it (consider moving your liquidity), and any that just dropped. If you have money in an inactive pool, it's working for nothing.

LP Grades — where votes keep the system healthy

Rankings merged here

Every vote is a resource. This grades each active pool on how much a vote there strengthens TLA — not how much it pays you (that's the Bounty Board) and not deposit opportunity (that's Rankings). methodology

The doctrine (SPEC-grading-and-dex-data): a pool's grade answers three questions — A. Is the pair intrinsically good? (depth, real volume, utilization) · B. Are the assets valuable to Terra? (fact-based asset rubric with a DAO-governed override layer — in build, not yet part of this grade) · C. Is it under-served? (support vs what it deserves — the "vote here" signal). What you see today is the interim grade from components A + C, computed page-side from live data with published weights; the full facts-only system replaces these inputs as its un-gameable sample accrues. Trust rule honored now: SkeletonSwap volume has no trustworthy source, so SS pools skip Utilization entirely (reweighted) rather than grade on suspect numbers.

Current interim weights (all live data): Depth 25% — DEX pool size, log-scaled ($500K+ = full marks). Deep pools make trading cheap, which is the whole point of directing liquidity. Utilization 25% — weekly volume ÷ liquidity over the last 4 completed epochs. A pool that turns its liquidity over is doing real work; an idle pool is decoration. Support balance 25% — the $ of TLA-staked liquidity behind each 1M VP of votes, vs the system median. A pool with far MORE liquidity per vote than typical is underpaid (LPs are carrying it — votes there strengthen the system); far LESS is overweighted (emissions flow with little liquidity behind them). Scored highest at balance, with the underpaid side penalized more gently than the overpaid side. Threshold safety 15% — headroom above the 1% bucket threshold a pool needs to keep earning. Trend 10% — the pool's real (price-neutral) liquidity direction across tracked epochs, duplicates removed. Grades: A+ ≥90 · A ≥80 · B ≥70 · C ≥55 · D ≥40 · F below. Single-asset pools skip Depth/Utilization (reweighted). This is an opinionated v1 — the weights are shown so you can argue with them.

Our Philosophy

Alliance DAO serves as a balancing force in TLA governance. We're not here to compete with Phoenix Directive or Votion — we're here to fill the natural gaps in the ve(3,3) incentive structure. Using data-driven analysis, we support LPs with real utility and strategic value, working alongside PD and Votion to help promising projects and pools grow. Together, we strengthen Terra's liquidity ecosystem.
